What's going on at DL&W

The Niagara Frontier Transportation Authority selected Savarino Companies as its preferred development partner for the redevelopment of the historic Delaware, Lackawanna & Western Railroad Terminal through a public Request for Proposals process in 2018-2019. Savarino’s vision for the redevelopment of this 100-year-old train shed structure is to create a year-round mixed-use public gathering destination anchored by a new light rail transit station that aims to foster community, culture, and commerce.

Savarino has teamed up with Project for Public Spaces to facilitate a city-wide public outreach and engagement process that will inform the development team’s design, placemaking and programming strategies. Preliminary plans for the redevelopment of the terminal include 80,000 square feet of indoor and 50,000 square feet of outdoor programing space for a mix of uses, including enhanced waterfront activation through placemaking, waterfront restaurant/bar, a public marketplace, commercial art spaces and public art installations, flexible community event venue, small business incubator, light manufacturing space, community gardens, and public recreational amenities.
